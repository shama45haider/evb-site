How We Determine Value

Every category is valued differently. Here's what actually moves the needle on your offer:

Jewelry & Gold

Gold is priced by weight and purity (karat). A 14K chain weighing 20 grams is worth a predictable amount based on the current spot price. Diamonds add value based on cut, color, clarity, and carat — and whether they're natural or lab-grown matters significantly. Designer jewelry (Tiffany, Cartier, Bulgari) commands a premium over scrap value because of brand recognition in the resale market.

Watches

Condition, reference number, and whether you have the original box and papers are the three biggest value drivers for watches. A Rolex Submariner with papers is worth considerably more than the same watch without. Movement condition is verified on every piece we buy.

Designer Bags

Authentication is step one. After that, condition is everything — hardware, stitching, interior, handles, and corners all factor in. Popular sizes and colorways in Chanel, Louis Vuitton, and Hermès hold value exceptionally well. Exotic skins (python, crocodile) require additional verification.

Sneakers & Streetwear

Deadstock (unworn, original box) commands the highest premium. Worn items still sell well if condition is solid and the piece is in demand. We track current StockX and secondary market pricing daily to make sure our offers reflect real-time demand.

How to Get the Best Price

  1. Bring documentation: Box, papers, receipts, authentication cards, and original tags all add value. Don't leave them at home.
  2. Clean the item: A clean item presents better. Wipe down leather goods, polish metal jewelry. First impressions affect perceived value.
  3. Know what you have: Research comparable sold listings on platforms like Vestiaire, StockX, or eBay before you come in. You don't need to negotiate, but knowing the market helps you evaluate our offer confidently.
  4. Timing matters for gold: Gold prices fluctuate daily. If spot prices are high, it's a good time to sell scrap and estate pieces.
  5. Don't low-ball yourself: The biggest mistake sellers make is accepting the first offer from anyone without shopping around. We're confident in our offers, but you should feel confident too.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an appointment?
No. Walk-ins are welcome during store hours — Sunday through Thursday 12:30–6:30 PM and Friday 12:30–6:00 PM. For large collections or estate pieces, texting ahead helps us prepare.
What if I don't accept the offer?
No problem. The appraisal is always free and there's no obligation to sell. You can walk out with your item and no hard feelings.
Do you buy items in poor condition?
Yes, depending on the category. Gold and silver scrap value is condition-independent. Designer and streetwear pieces in poor condition are evaluated case by case — some still have strong resale demand regardless of wear.
Do you authenticate before buying?
Always. We authenticate every piece before making an offer. We do not buy items we cannot verify as genuine.
How do you compare to pawn shops?
We are not a pawn shop. We make outright purchase offers — we don't offer loans against items. Our offers reflect secondary market value, not the deeply discounted rates typical of pawn loan collateral.
Can I sell multiple items at once?
Yes. Bring in as many pieces as you'd like. Estate sales, closet cleanouts, and collection liquidations are welcome. For very large lots, texting us in advance helps us set aside time.
