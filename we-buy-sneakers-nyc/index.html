How We Authenticate

How Do We Authenticate Sneakers?

Every pair is inspected in front of you at the counter.

Fake sneakers flood the market. We check brand-specific details before making any offer:

  • Materials & Build — leather quality, stitching, glue lines, midsole foam
  • Labels & Tags — size tag fonts, UPC codes, production date formatting
  • Box & Packaging — label matching, tissue paper, lace bags when present
  • Brand Markers — Nike swoosh shape, Jordan wings logo, Yeezy boost pellets
  • Cross-Reference — compared against retail releases and resale databases

If it doesn't pass, we'll tell you why. If it does, you get a cash offer on the spot.

FAQ

Common Questions

Quick answers about selling sneakers at East Village Buyers.

Do I need an appointment?
Walk-ins welcome at 39 Avenue A. For large collections, text photos to 917-608-8939 first.
How do I get paid?
Same-day cash on the spot when you accept our offer.
Can I text photos for a quote?
Yes — text clear photos to 917-608-8939 for a rough estimate. Final offers confirmed in person.
What affects sneaker value?
Authenticity, condition, rarity, size, colorway, and current NYC resale demand.
Does the original box matter?
OG box with matching label increases the offer but is not required.
Do you buy used sneakers?
Yes — gently worn pairs are welcome when condition and demand support resale.
Which sizes sell best?
Sizes 8–12 have the highest demand. We still buy outside that range.
Do you buy fakes or replicas?
No. We only buy and sell authentic sneakers.
Do you buy from StockX or GOAT?
Yes — we authenticate every pair regardless of where you bought them.
Can I trade sneakers?
Yes — sell for cash or trade toward something in our inventory.
Which Jordans have the most value?
Jordan 1, 3, 4, and 11 retros in OG colorways and limited collabs.
Do you buy yellowed soles?
Depends on the model. Some vintage pairs with yellowing still carry premium.
Can I sell multiple pairs at once?
Yes — bring as many as you like. We review them all in one visit.
Where are you & what are your hours?

Are you licensed?
Yes — NYC DCA license #2070477 (Vintage USA Inc).
How quickly do prices drop after release?
General releases lose value fast. Limited pairs often gain value over time.
