FAQ

Common Questions

Quick answers about selling silver at East Village Buyers.

Do I need an appointment?
Walk-ins welcome. For large flatware collections or estate silver, text photos to 917-608-8939 ahead so we can prepare.
How do you determine my silver's value?
We weigh your silver, verify purity (acid test + hallmark check), and calculate against the live spot price of silver at the time of your visit.
Does tarnish affect the value?
No. Tarnish is surface oxidation and has zero impact on silver content or value. Bring it as-is.
How do I know if my flatware is sterling or plated?
Look for hallmarks: sterling pieces are stamped ".925," "STERLING," or "925." Silver-plated pieces say "EPNS," "Silver Plate," or have no precious metal stamps. We test everything at the counter.
Can I sell a partial flatware set?
Yes — you don't need a complete set. Individual pieces, partial sets, and even single serving spoons all have melt value if they're sterling.
Do you buy Tiffany silver?
Yes — Tiffany & Co. silver often carries a significant premium above melt based on the piece, condition, and resale demand.
Do you buy Chrome Hearts silver?
Yes — Chrome Hearts is highly collectible and we pay strong premiums for authenticated pieces. We verify at the counter.
What are pre-1964 US coins worth?
Dimes, quarters, and half-dollars minted before 1964 are 90% silver. We buy rolls and bags at competitive rates based on live spot pricing.
Can I text photos for a quote?
Yes — text clear photos to 917-608-8939 for a rough estimate before coming in. Weight is required for a final offer.
How do I get paid?
Same-day cash or check for larger transactions. Payment immediately after you accept the offer — no waiting.
Do you buy silver tea sets?
Yes — tea sets, coffee sets, and full sterling silver serving sets are welcome. Bring the whole set for the best offer.
Are you licensed?
Yes — NYC DCA license #2070477 (Vintage USA Inc, DBA East Village Buyers).
Do you buy silver-plated items?
Silver-plated items have a very thin silver coating with minimal silver content — we typically pass on these. If you're unsure, bring it in and we'll test it.
