How We Check the Value of Your Silver

Nothing happens behind a curtain. Here's the exact process we run on every piece, right at the counter, while you watch.

Done in under 5 minutes

Step 1

Look It Over

We check for a stamp like 925, 900, or "sterling," which gives us a starting point before any testing.

Step 2

Run an Acid Test

A tiny scratch and a drop of testing solution tells us fast if the silver is really what it says it is.

Step 3

Confirm Electronically

For pieces we don't want to mark or scratch, an electronic reader checks purity with no damage at all.

Step 4

Weigh & Check Live Price

We weigh everything in grams and multiply that against today's live silver price.

FAQ

Silver Selling Questions, Answered Straight

The questions we hear most, including the ones people are usually too polite to ask out loud.

Is East Village Buyers a pawn shop?
No. A pawn shop lends you money and holds your item until you pay it back. We buy your silver outright. You get cash, we get the silver, and that's the whole deal — nothing to pay off later.
How much is my silver worth right now?
It comes down to purity and weight, times today's silver price. Check the live price widget above for a rough idea, then bring your piece in for an exact number.
What is sterling silver worth per gram?
Sterling is 92.5% pure silver, so it's worth about 92.5% of the day's spot price per gram. That percent stays the same, but the dollar amount changes with the market every day.
Do you test my silver in front of me?
Every time, no exceptions. We run an acid test and an electronic check right at the counter so you can watch the whole thing before we talk numbers.
Does tarnish lower the value?
No. Tarnish only affects the surface, not the silver underneath. We price by weight and purity, so tarnished pieces sell for the same as shiny ones.
Do you buy broken or tangled chains?
Yes. A snapped clasp or a knotted mess doesn't change how much silver is inside, so it doesn't change what we pay.
Do I need a receipt to sell silver?
No receipt needed. We only care what the piece is made of, not where it came from. You will need a valid government ID, which is standard for this kind of sale in New York.
Do you buy silver-plated items?
Plated pieces have a very thin coat of silver over another metal, so there's almost nothing to pay for and we usually pass. If you're not sure what you have, bring it in and we'll test it for free.
Is there a limit on how much silver you'll buy?
No. We've bought everything from one ring to a full box of flatware. For a big collection, text photos to 917-608-8939 ahead of time so we can plan for it.
How fast do I get paid?
Same day. As soon as you say yes to our offer, you're paid before you leave the counter.
Do I need an appointment?
Walk-ins are always welcome. If you're bringing a large or valuable collection, a heads-up text helps us prepare, though it's not required.

Yes, text clear photos to 917-608-8939 for a rough idea. We'll still need to weigh and test the piece in person for a final number.
