How We Authenticate Chrome Hearts — What Fakes Always Get Wrong

Chrome Hearts fakes have gotten scary good. At our counter on Avenue A, we see replica rings, crosses, and hoodies every week. Here is what we actually look for — and why a receipt alone does not guarantee anything.

The Details Fakes Keep Missing

Authentic Chrome Hearts has a specific weight, finish, and craftsmanship you can feel immediately. When we buy Chrome Hearts in NYC, we go way beyond a quick glance at a logo.

  • Font and spacing on engravings: Replicas often use slightly off letterforms or uneven stamping depth.
  • Hardware weight and finish: Real pieces feel substantial. Cheap plating and hollow builds are instant red flags.
  • Leather and cross patch quality: On apparel, stitch density, patch placement, and tag fonts tell the real story.

What Hurts Your Resale Value

Heavy scratches, stretched-out hoodies, missing tags, and modified sizing all lower your offer. We still buy worn Chrome Hearts — but pristine pieces always command top dollar in the NYC resale market.
