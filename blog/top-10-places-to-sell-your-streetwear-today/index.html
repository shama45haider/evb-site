Top 10 Places to Sell Your Streetwear Today

You've got Supreme box logos, BAPE hoodies, Off-White pieces, or vintage Nike sitting in your closet. You want to turn them into cash. The question is where — because not every option is equal. Some pay fast but low. Some pay high but take weeks. Some take a cut you didn't expect.

Here's a straight ranking of the 10 best places to sell streetwear today, based on payout, speed, and how much hassle is involved.

#2
Grailed
Online Marketplace · Peer-to-Peer

Grailed is the go-to platform for selling streetwear and designer menswear directly to buyers. The audience understands value — you won't have to explain why a vintage Supreme work jacket costs what it does. The ceiling on prices is high for the right pieces. The downsides: you need to photograph, list, negotiate, ship, and wait. Grailed takes a commission on each sale. For rare or highly desirable items where you have time, it's worth it.

High ceiling for rare pieces Takes days or weeks Platform fee + payment processing
#3
StockX
Authenticated Resale · Sneakers & Streetwear

StockX works like a stock market for sneakers and streetwear. You list at an ask price, a buyer accepts, you ship, StockX authenticates, and they send you money. It's legitimate and trusted, but the process takes time and StockX takes a seller fee that increases if you're a new seller. Best for in-demand sneakers and Supreme drops with clear market prices. Not ideal if you need cash fast.

Trusted authentication Strong prices for in-demand items Seller fees (9–12%+) Takes 1–2+ weeks

#4
GOAT
Authenticated Resale · Sneakers Focus

GOAT is primarily a sneaker platform but has expanded into apparel. Similar model to StockX — list, ship, authenticate, get paid. GOAT has a large buyer base and strong authentication reputation. Their fees are comparable to StockX. If you're selling high-value deadstock sneakers, GOAT and StockX are your two best online options. For apparel, Grailed is usually better.

Great for deadstock sneakers Seller commission fees Slow payout timeline
#5
eBay
General Marketplace · Auction or Fixed Price

eBay has a massive buyer base and works well for vintage or niche streetwear that doesn't have a clear price on StockX or Grailed. The auction format can drive prices up unexpectedly for highly sought-after pieces. The downside is the effort involved — listing, photographing, shipping, dealing with returns, and eBay's seller fees. Good for rare vintage pieces; less ideal for common resale items that have cleaner comps elsewhere.

Huge buyer base Good for rare vintage Time-intensive Returns and disputes
#6
Depop
Social Marketplace · Younger Audience

Depop skews younger and works well for Y2K, vintage, and fast-fashion-adjacent streetwear. If your pieces have aesthetic appeal — interesting graphics, a cool era, unique colorways — Depop buyers respond to that. It's less suited to authenticated luxury streetwear. The platform charges a fee, and like all peer-to-peer platforms, you need to manage listings and shipping yourself.

Great for vintage and Y2K Younger buyer base Platform fees
#7
Round Two NYC
In-Store Reseller · Lower East Side

Round Two is one of the most respected streetwear resale shops in the country. They buy in-store in NYC. The upside is speed — walk in, get an offer. The downside is that their offers reflect the wholesale side of the market: they need room to mark up for resale. For very specific items they're actively looking for, the price can be solid. For most things, you'll get more from a direct buyer or online.

Walk-in, fast Wholesale offers Selective on what they buy
#8
Stadium Goods
Consignment · SoHo NYC

Stadium Goods takes streetwear and sneakers on consignment — meaning they list your item, sell it, and take a percentage. You get paid after the item sells, not immediately. For high-value sneakers that will definitely move, the consignment model can yield good numbers. But you're waiting, and their cut is significant. Not the move if you need cash today.

Good prices for sneakers Consignment — paid after sale High commission percentage
#9
Facebook Marketplace
Local Peer-to-Peer · No Fees

Facebook Marketplace has no seller fees, which is its main advantage. In NYC you can find local buyers and avoid shipping entirely. The downsides are real: you'll deal with low-ballers, flakes, and the logistics of meetups in a busy city. Works best for lower-value items where platform fees would eat into your profit more than the hassle is worth. For Supreme or BAPE, you're better off somewhere with a more serious buyer pool.

No platform fees Low-ballers and flakes Safety concerns for meetups
#10
Poshmark
Social Marketplace · Bundled Listings

Poshmark works well if you're clearing out a whole closet rather than selling one or two pieces — buyers can bundle items into a single order, which helps move lower-value basics alongside your grails. The tradeoff is a steep flat commission on lower-priced sales and the same listing, shipping, and waiting that comes with any peer-to-peer platform. Best for volume sellers; not the fastest way to get cash for a single hyped piece.

Good for bundling multiple items Steep commission on lower-priced items Listing and shipping required
