Top 10 Best Streetwear Stores to Visit in NYC

New York City has always been the center of streetwear culture in America. From the Lafayette Street corridor to the East Village, the city has blocks dedicated to the culture — and a handful of stores that have defined what streetwear even means. Whether you're shopping, collecting, or selling, here are the 10 best streetwear stores worth visiting in NYC right now.

#1
Supreme
274 Lafayette St, SoHo

The original. Supreme's flagship on Lafayette has been the epicenter of New York streetwear since 1994. Thursday drops still draw lines around the block. If you've never been, it's a pilgrimage worth making — though most serious collectors know that the real value is in what you find after the hype dies down, not during it. Box logos, work jackets, and accessories hold their value better than almost anything else in the resale market.

#2
Kith
233 Lafayette St, SoHo

Ronnie Fieg turned a sneaker shop into a lifestyle brand with global reach. Kith NYC carries its own label alongside Nike, New Balance, Adidas, and a curated selection of brands that nobody else stocks. The flagship is spacious and well-merchandised — it doesn't feel like a hypebeast trap, it feels like a real store. Kith collaborations consistently resell above retail, particularly New Balance and ASICS collabs.

#3
A Bathing Ape (BAPE)
91 Greene St, SoHo

BAPE's SoHo location is one of the brand's best stores in the world. The shark hoodies, camo prints, and Baby Milo graphics that defined 2000s streetwear still move serious money on the resale market. Vintage BAPE — especially pieces from the early 2000s — is some of the most sought-after streetwear in existence. Their in-store exclusives are worth checking regularly.

#4
Dover Street Market
160 Lexington Ave, Midtown

DSM is the closest thing NYC has to a luxury streetwear department store. Comme des Garçons, Sacai, Craig Green, and a rotating cast of emerging designers share space with Nike and Supreme collaborations. It's as much an art installation as a retail store. What you find here is editorial-level curation — and pieces that appreciate rather than depreciate if you hold them.

#5
Union New York
172 Spring St, SoHo

One of the most influential streetwear stores in the world that most people outside the culture have never heard of. Union has been around since 1989 and their Jordan Brand collaborations have become some of the most valuable sneaker releases of the last decade. Chris Gibbs, who runs Union LA, is as respected as anyone in the game. Small shop, massive influence.

#6
Extra Butter
Various NYC locations

Extra Butter started in Queens and has grown into one of the most respected independent sneaker and streetwear retailers in the country. Their storytelling approach to product drops — each release built around a cinematic theme — has made them a destination beyond just the product. Collab sneakers with Reebok, New Balance, and ASICS are consistently well-executed and have strong aftermarket value.

#7
Stadium Goods
47 Howard St, SoHo

If you want to see the full landscape of what's available on the resale market — in one room, authenticated, displayed well — Stadium Goods is the place. It's a consignment and resale shop at heart, but executed at a level that makes it feel like a museum. Jordans, Yeezys, vintage Nike, and limited collabs fill the walls floor to ceiling. Great place to research what things are actually worth before you sell elsewhere.

#8
Bodega
Hidden entrance, Lower East Side

Bodega's New York outpost carries on the tradition of the original Boston store — hidden entrance, curated selection, and a genuine insider feel. They focus on elevated basics, technical outerwear, and sneakers from brands like New Balance, Saucony, and Salomon. Less hype, more craft. Their own collaborations are consistently some of the most tasteful in the market.

#9
Round Two NYC
Lower East Side

Round Two is where the serious resellers and collectors go. Part vintage shop, part sneaker reseller, part streetwear archive — their LES location stocks vintage Supreme, Polo, Nike, and archive pieces that you simply won't find anywhere else in the city. If you're hunting for something specific from 2002, this is your best shot. They also buy — though their offers tend to reflect the wholesale side of the market.

Tips for Shopping Streetwear in NYC

  • Check drop calendars before you go. Supreme drops Thursday mornings. Kith and others announce via Instagram and their apps. Showing up blind means missing the good stuff.
  • Bring cash to vintage shops. Round Two and smaller archive spots often prefer cash and may offer better deals off-card.
  • Authenticate before you buy resale. Counterfeit Supreme, Chrome Hearts, and Off-White are everywhere. If you're spending real money, verify authenticity with someone who knows the product.
  • Know your resale values. Stadium Goods and StockX are good references for current market prices. Don't overpay at a shop for something that's sitting on StockX for less.
  • Sell what you're not wearing. Dead stock in your closet is money. If you haven't touched a piece in a year, bring it to us — you'll leave with cash same day.

The East Village Streetwear Scene

The East Village has its own streetwear identity that's separate from SoHo. Avenue A, St. Marks Place, and the surrounding blocks have a mix of vintage stores, independent boutiques, and the kind of lived-in thrift culture that SoHo lost when rents tripled. It's where you find things you didn't know you were looking for.
