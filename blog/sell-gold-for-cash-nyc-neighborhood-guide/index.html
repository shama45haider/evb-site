What Determines Your Cash Offer for Gold

Three factors drive every gold offer. Understanding them puts you in a better position before you walk in anywhere.

Karat Purity

Pure gold is 24 karat. Most jewelry sold in the US is 14 karat (58.3% pure), 18 karat (75% pure), or occasionally 10 karat (41.7% pure). Higher karat means a higher per-gram payout. We test every piece individually — we don't assume based on stamps alone.

Weight

Weight is measured in grams on certified digital scales calibrated to a hundredth of a gram. Note: carats measure gemstones, not gold weight — these are two completely different units. A heavier piece pays more, all else equal.

Live Spot Price

The spot price is the global market price for one troy ounce of pure gold, and it moves every few seconds during market hours. Transparent buyers tie their payouts to a live percentage of spot price. We show you the number we're using so you can verify it yourself on any financial website.

Common Mistakes NYC Sellers Make

  • Accepting the first offer without comparing transparent, local buyers
  • Grouping all gold together — this prevents per-piece karat verification and can lower your total payout
  • Throwing away broken or damaged pieces — they retain 100% of their melt value
  • Not asking what percentage of spot price the buyer offers — this number is the most important one to know

Frequently Asked Questions

Where can I sell gold for cash near 10009?
East Village Buyers at 39 Avenue A is your closest option. We provide same-day cash payouts, visible testing, and live spot pricing — right near Tompkins Square Park.
Do I need an appointment to sell gold?
No — walk-ins are always welcome. Most transactions complete in 15–30 minutes. If you have a large collection, texting us photos ahead of time helps us prepare.
How does East Village Buyers test gold?
We use either an acid test or an electronic precious metals tester depending on the piece. Both methods identify karat purity accurately and happen visibly at the counter.
Can I sell broken gold jewelry?
Yes. Broken chains, bent rings, single earrings, and damaged pieces all receive the same per-gram rate as pristine items. Don't throw anything away before you talk to us.
What's the difference between a gold buyer and a pawn shop?
Pawn shops loan money against your items with interest — you get your stuff back only if you repay the loan. Gold buyers purchase outright for cash. We typically offer stronger payouts because there's no loan structure involved.
Do I need ID to sell gold in NYC?
Yes. New York State law requires a government-issued photo ID for any precious metal transaction. A driver's license, state ID, or passport all work.
Can I sell gold coins?
Yes. American Eagles, Maple Leafs, Krugerrands, and European sovereigns are all purchased. We consider numismatic (collector) value separately from melt value when applicable.
Where can Williamsburg residents sell gold?
Take the L train to 1st Ave or cross the Williamsburg Bridge — East Village Buyers is one of Manhattan's closest and most transparent options for Brooklyn residents.
What gold items have the most value?
Heavy 18k or 22k pieces, gold coins, and large solid chains command the highest values. Hollow chains and gold-plated items contain less actual gold and pay accordingly.
