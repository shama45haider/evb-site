Sell Your Silver Jewelry in East Village NYC — Get Top Dollar with Trusted Local Buyers

If you have silver jewelry, coins, silverware, or designer silver pieces sitting around collecting dust, you're sitting on more cash than you might think. Whether you have a heavy box of tarnished chains, a set of sterling silverware passed down from a relative, or a prized Chrome Hearts ring you're ready to part with — we want to buy it. This guide covers everything you need to know before walking in.

The East Village is one of Manhattan's most vibrant neighborhoods, and East Village Buyers has built a reputation as the go-to local shop for fast, fair cash offers. Unlike pawnshops or mail-in services, we give you a face-to-face, transparent offer on the spot — no waiting, no guessing, no lowball tactics.

We buy silver from all walks of life: jewelry collectors, estate liquidators, people clearing out a parent's home, and everyday New Yorkers who simply need fast cash. Walk-ins are always welcome — no appointment needed.

What Types of Silver Can You Sell?

We buy a wide range of silver items, including:

  • Sterling silver jewelry — necklaces, bracelets, rings, earrings, and pendants stamped .925 or "Sterling"
  • Silver coins & bullion — American Silver Eagles, Morgan dollars, junk silver, and silver bars
  • Sterling silverware & flatware — full sets or individual pieces; condition doesn't need to be perfect
  • Designer silver — Chrome Hearts, Tiffany & Co., David Yurman, Georg Jensen, and other premium brands
  • Damaged or broken silver — broken chains, bent rings, incomplete sets — we still buy it for scrap value

Note on silver-plated items: Silver-plated pieces (items with only a thin silver coating over a base metal) generally hold little to no scrap value, as the silver content is negligible. If your piece is magnetic, it's likely plated. If it's stamped 925 or "Sterling," you've got real value.

How Silver Value Is Determined

When we evaluate your silver, we look at three key factors:

  • Purity — Sterling silver is 92.5% pure silver, marked with "925," "Sterling," or "STER." We test any unmarked pieces in-house.
  • Weight — Silver is priced by the troy ounce. The heavier your piece, the more it's worth in scrap.
  • Spot price — Silver's market price fluctuates daily. We offer competitive rates tied to the current silver spot price.
  • Brand & collectability — Designer pieces from Chrome Hearts, Tiffany, or David Yurman command a premium above raw melt value because of their resale demand.

Tips to Get the Best Price for Your Silver

  • Bring any original packaging, certificates of authenticity, or receipts for designer pieces — it helps us authenticate and increases your offer.
  • Don't clean or polish your silver before bringing it in. Harsh polishes can damage hallmarks and reduce value.
  • Bring everything at once — even small pieces add up by weight, and larger lots give us more to work with.
  • Text us photos in advance at 917-608-8939 to get a ballpark estimate before you walk in.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an appointment to sell my silver?
No — walk-ins are always welcome. We're open Sunday through Thursday 12:30—6:30 PM and Friday 12:30—6:00 PM. We're closed on Saturdays.
What if my silver isn't stamped or marked?
No problem. We can test unmarked silver in-house using acid and electronic testing to determine its purity. Bring it in and we'll tell you exactly what you have.
Do you buy silver-plated items?
Unfortunately, silver-plated items (copper or brass with a thin silver coat) have very minimal silver content and typically don't hold scrap value. However, if your piece is from a notable designer, we may still be interested — bring it in and we'll take a look.
Do you buy damaged or broken silver?
Yes. Broken chains, bent rings, cracked pendants — scrap silver is still worth good money by weight. Don't toss it; bring it in.
How much is my silver worth?
It depends on the weight and current silver spot price. Text us photos at 917-608-8939 for a quick estimate, or walk in for a full in-person appraisal.
