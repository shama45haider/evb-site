Where in NYC Can I Sell Electronics?

Got an old iPhone sitting in a drawer? A laptop you upgraded past? A camera you stopped using? New York City has plenty of places that will take your electronics off your hands — but they're not all equal. Some will pay you fairly in cash the same day. Others will nickel-and-dime you with fees, low-ball you, or make you wait weeks to get paid.

Here's a straight breakdown of every realistic option for selling electronics in NYC, what each one is good for, and where to go if you want the most cash with the least hassle.

Trade-In
Apple Trade-In / Carrier Trade-Ins
Convenient · Low offers · Store credit only

Apple, Verizon, AT&T, and T-Mobile all run trade-in programs. The upside is convenience — you're already there upgrading your phone. The downside is that you get store credit, not cash, and the values are typically significantly below what you'd get from a direct buyer. If you're buying a new device anyway and the math works out, it's fine. If you want actual money in your hand, it's not the move.

  • Simple and fast if you're upgrading anyway
  • Store credit only — not cash
  • Offers are usually well below market value
  • Only works if you're buying something new from them

Resale Platform
eBay / Swappa / Facebook Marketplace
Higher ceiling · More work · Slower

Selling directly to another person online can get you more money for the right items — especially collectible or hard-to-find tech. But it takes time: photographing, listing, answering messages, waiting for a buyer, packing, shipping, and dealing with returns or disputes. eBay takes a cut. Shipping adds risk. And Facebook Marketplace meetups in NYC require dealing with flakes and scheduling strangers. For a MacBook Pro or a professional camera kit, it can be worth it. For a midrange phone, it's usually not worth the trouble.

  • Potentially highest offer for in-demand or specialty items
  • Takes days or weeks to sell
  • Platform fees, shipping costs, and return risk
  • Time-intensive to list and manage
Mail-In
Decluttr / ItsWorthMore / SellCell
Convenient · Locked-in quotes · Slow payout

Mail-in services let you get a quote online, ship your device, and get paid later. The quoted price is locked in — but only if the device matches your description exactly. If they inspect and downgrade your condition rating, the offer drops before you see the money. Payout typically takes 5–10 business days after they receive the device. For people outside NYC or who hate in-person transactions, it works. For anyone who wants fast cash, it's not ideal.

  • No need to leave home
  • Upfront online quote
  • Quotes drop if condition doesn't match exactly
  • 1–2 week turnaround from shipping to payment
  • You're without your device for the whole process
Trade-In
GameStop
Phones, consoles & games · Low trade-in values

GameStop accepts gaming consoles, games, controllers, phones, and tablets. Their trade-in values are notoriously low — you'll typically get significantly more selling to a direct buyer. They also push heavily toward store credit rather than cash. Worth checking their current prices if you have a very specific item they're currently buying well, but generally not competitive on value.

  • Accepts consoles and games without condition scrutiny
  • Trade-in values are consistently below market
  • Pushes store credit over cash

How Much Will I Get for My Electronics?

The honest answer: it depends on the specific device, its condition, and current market demand. A recent iPhone in great condition with a working battery will get you a strong offer. An older model with a cracked screen and swollen battery will get you much less.

The biggest factors that affect your offer:

  • Model and storage size — a 256GB iPhone 15 Pro Max is worth more than a 128GB iPhone 14
  • Condition — screen, body, battery health (for phones), and whether everything works
  • Unlocked vs. carrier-locked — unlocked devices command higher offers
  • Original accessories — having the original charger, case, or box adds a small amount for some items
  • Market demand — electronics depreciate over time; selling sooner after a device launch gets you more
