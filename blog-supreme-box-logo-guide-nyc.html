Supreme Box Logo Season Guide — Which Years Are Still Worth Money?

Everyone knows a Supreme box logo is iconic. But not every season holds the same value. If you are sitting on old Supreme in your closet, some pieces are still serious money — and others have cooled off hard.

Which Supreme Box Logos Still Move

At East Village Buyers we buy and sell Supreme every week. The box logo market is not one price fits all. Colorway, season, condition, and whether you still have the original tags all change what we can pay on the spot.

  • Early 2000s and classic red box logos: Still among the most requested pieces we buy in NYC.
  • Rare colorways (camo, orange, heather grey): Often outperform standard black or navy on the resale market.
  • Recent seasons (2020+): Can still sell well, but only if condition is clean and hype is still active.

What We Check Before We Buy

When you walk in to sell Supreme, we look at print cracking, collar stretching, pit stains, and tag legitimacy. We also track what is actually selling right now — not what sold three years ago on StockX.
