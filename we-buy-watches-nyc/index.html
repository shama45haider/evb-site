Good To Know

You Don't Need to Be a Watch Expert

Most people who bring in a watch have no idea what it's really worth, and that's completely normal. We look at every watch with you standing right there, and we walk you through what we're checking so you understand exactly how we got your number. Whether it's a Rolex, an Omega, a family heirloom, or a watch you're not sure about, we'll tell you what it's worth before you decide to sell anything.

How We Check the Value of Your Watch

Nothing happens behind a curtain. Here's the exact process we run on every watch, right at the counter, while you watch.

Done in under 10 minutes

Step 1

Look It Over

We check the brand, model, case, dial, and bracelet to see what we're working with.

Step 2

Verify the Serial

We match the serial and model numbers to confirm the watch is genuine, using papers if you have them.

Step 3

Check the Movement

We look at how the movement runs and note any service history or repairs it may need.

Step 4

Make You an Offer

We weigh the brand, condition, and demand, then give you a straight cash offer on the spot.

No Guesswork

Watch values change with the brand, model, and market demand, so there's no single price list we can put on a page. Bring your watch in and we'll give you a real number based on what it is right now, not a guess.

FAQ

Watch Selling Questions, Answered Straight

The questions we hear most, including the ones people are usually too polite to ask out loud.

Is East Village Buyers a pawn shop?
No. A pawn shop lends you money and holds your watch until you pay it back. We buy your watch outright. You get cash, we get the watch, and that's the whole deal — nothing to pay off later.
How much is my watch worth right now?
It depends on the brand, model, condition, and current demand. Bring it in and we'll check it in person for an exact number.
Do you check my watch in front of me?
Every time, no exceptions. We check the case, dial, movement, and serial number right at the counter so you can watch the whole process before we talk numbers.
Do I need box and papers to sell my watch?
No, but they help. A full set usually gets a higher offer since it's easier for us to resell. We'll still make a fair offer without them.
Which Rolex models hold the most value?
Submariner, Daytona, and GMT-Master II consistently hold the strongest resale value.
Do you buy watches that don't run?
Yes. A non-running watch still has real value, especially from brands like Rolex, Omega, and Patek Philippe. We factor the cost of service into the offer.
Do you buy vintage watches?
Yes — vintage Rolex, Omega, Grand Seiko, and other Swiss and Japanese watches with a readable serial number.
Do you buy ladies' watches?
Yes — Tiffany & Co, Cartier Tank, Rolex Datejust, Omega Constellation, and other ladies' luxury pieces.
Does the original bracelet matter?
Yes. An original bracelet is worth more than an aftermarket strap. Bring it separately if it's not on the watch.
Do you buy fake or replica watches?
No. We only buy authentic, verified timepieces.
Do you buy quartz watches?
Yes. Mechanical movements tend to hold more value, but quartz watches from Cartier, TAG Heuer, and Tiffany still sell well.
Is there a limit on how many watches you'll buy?
No. We buy anywhere from a single watch to a full collection. Text photos to 917-608-8939 ahead of time for a large collection.
How fast do I get paid?
Same day. As soon as you accept our offer, you're paid before you leave the counter.
Do I need an appointment?
Walk-ins are always welcome. For a high-value piece or a large collection, a heads-up text helps us prepare.
Are you licensed?
Yes — NYC DCA license #2070477 (Vintage USA Inc, DBA East Village Buyers).
